All About Rabbits & Bunnies


Intro to Rabbits

In the world, there are over 50 different breeds of Rabbits. They come in many shapes, sizes, colors, and live in various environments. They are one of the most popular pets that people have. Rabbits are adorable small mammals that are known for their soft fur, long ears, fluffy ball tails and cuteness! Mammals are warm-blood animals with backbones. Rabbits are social, charming, and affectionate beings that has a universal appeal. These creatures are herbivores and considered part of the leporidae family. The terms, rabbits and bunnies, are used interchangebly but they refer to the same thing. To clarify, bunnies are the young version of rabbits.

Female and male rabbits differ in name. Female rabbits are called doe and male ones are called bucks. The babies of these creatures are called kits. A unique trait of kits is that they are born without fur and can live on their own at just 5 weeks old! Rabbits can even make their own family at 2 to 3 months old! Rabbits usually last for 5 to 8 years but can go up to about 12 years if they are a purebred.

Where do Rabbits Usually Live?

Rabbits often live in family groups and some live in underground burrows or rabbit holes. Rabbits, as said earlier, live in various different environments all around the world.
These include:

  • Meadows
  • Woods
  • Forests
  • Grasslands
  • Deserts
  • Wetlands

Fun Facts!

  1. They are masters of hearing. They can turn their ears 180 degrees.
  2. Rabbits have almost 360 degree vision.
  3. Carrots aren't the best food choice for them because they are high in sugar.
  4. Rabbits are very social and enjoy being around people.
  5. Rabbits eat their own droppings for nutrition.
  6. When they're happy, they do what is called a "binky". It is when they hop in the air, spinning, and kicking their feet.
